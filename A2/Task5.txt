Discussion
==========
	
In general, BLEU score increases as we give the alignment model more training
data (i.e. as we increase Number of Sentences). The general pattern is:
 - 10000 model is much better than 1000 model
 - 15000 model is a little better than 10000 model
 - 30000 and 15000 models are on average roughly identical

This makes sense: we expect that as we give the model more training data, it
gets a more accurate representation of the underlying distribution we are trying
to model. But we see diminishing returns, because we soon begin to reach the
limit of this simplistic model's capabilities.

In general, as N increases, BLEU scores decrease. This is also expected: as N
increases, we consider larger and larger n-grams, meaning that the automatic
translation must match the references more and more closely in order to get
similar scores.

Discussion of Reference Sentences
=================================

In general, the Hansard translations are more accurate/correct than the Google
translations. For example, consider this sentence:

-French: Les deputes liberaux sont nombreux a representer des circonscription rurales.
-Hansard English: Many Liberal members come from rural ridings.
-Google English: The liberal deputies are many represent a rural constituency.

Note that the Google translation isn't even grammatical, and doesn't convey the
correct meaning.

On the other hand, the Google translations are often much more literal than the
Hansard translations. For example:

-French: C'est le comble du ridicule
-Hansard English: I cannot imagine anything so ridiculous.
-Google English: It is the height of ridicule

The Google translation in this case is a much more literal or "exact"
translation (although the Hansard translation is a more natural English
phrasing). If we used only the Hansard reference, which is very liberal in its
rephrasing, we may unfairly penalize a perfectly good translation simply because
it remained faithful to the original French.

Advantages of using more than two references: there are many ways of phrasing a
sentence, and so having a variety of diverse reference phrasings means we are
less likely to penalize the machine translation system simply because it chose a
phrasing which was different from (but just as good as) the reference translations.

Disadvantages of using more than two references: if we add too many references,
then we risk losing our ability to properly guage fluency: the machine
translation system can produce ungrammatical franken-sentences in which the
parts don't fit together, but as long as each part can be found in one of the
reference sentences, the translation will get a good score.
